Welcome to our beta site and modules! This website and the modules are under development and we welcome any feedback on this website and on the individual modules.

Modules

A list of modules developed for the project are listed here. The first group of modules are the completed courses. The second group of modules are still in development.

All of the completed modules are also available in our github repository

Available Modules

Clicking on the title of module will present a brief overview of the module. Below the overview, clicking on the "view" button will allow you to see the various parts of the module, sample the modules, or download pieces of the topic.

  • Biomedical Big Data Science
    BDK01
    Overview of biomedical big data science and biomedical, clinical, and translational research.
  • Introduction to Big Data in Biology and Medicine
    BDK02
    Examples of issues of measurement and scale; avoid “big data hubris” with examples from Google Flu Trends
  • Ethical Issues in Use of Big Data
    BDK03
    Discussion of the major ethical issues surrounding the use of data
  • Clinical Data Standards Related to Big Data
    BDK04
    Overview of clinical data standards related to big data.
  • Basic Research Data Standards
    BDK05
    Standards for biomedical data, participating in communities that develop them, aiding reproducibility
  • Public Health and Big Data
    BDK06
    Overview of public health and its use of big data
  • Team Science
    BDK07
    “Team Science” will define team science in the context of modern bioinformatics research. The module will highlight some of the most pressing considerations facing team science, and provide a basic framework for understanding and establishing high functioning teams.
  • Secondary Use (Reuse) of Clinical Data
    BDK08
    Overview of the secondary use (or re-use) of clinical data
  • Publication and Peer Review
    BDK09
    Overview of the process of publication of scientific data and information, including peer review, journal literature, and limitations of the scientific literature
  • Information Retrieval
    BDK10
    Overview of information retrieval (search) for knowledge-based information.
  • Data annotation and curation
    BDK12
    Data preparation, developing standardized quality assurance processes and pipelines
  • Ontologies 101
    BDK14
    Introduction to OWL2 and data reasoning
  • Data metadata and provenance
    BDK15
    Data set descriptions, ISA-TAB, and W3C standards for versioning, provenance, and attribution
  • Semantic data Interoperability
    BDK16
    Tools to semantically integrate data
  • Choice of Algorithms and Algorithm Dynamics
    BDK17
    Current approaches for modeling and mining Big Data; Streaming and parallel algorithms; Optimization considerations
  • Visualization and Interpretation
    BDK18
    Streaming data visualization; Visual data mining; Visual search and recommendation; Big data storytelling using visualization; Scalable parallel visualization methods; Advanced hardware and architecture for data visualization and analysis; Human computer interaction
  • Replication, Validation and the spectrum of Reproducibility
    BDK19
    The spectrum of reproducibility; Replication vs. Validation; Data Integrity
  • Regulatory Issues in Big Data for Genomics and Health
    BDK20
    Research regulations; Privacy regulations; Compliance
  • Hosting data dissemination and data stewardship workshops
    BDK21
    Workshop materials and documentation for leading workshops pertaining to NIH guidelines, best practices for data management, data standards, data linking, public repositories, and your scholarly footprint
  • Guidelines for Reporting, Publications, and Data Sharing
    BDK22
    Review of current policies and how they pertain to different organizations; Data sharing versus data reuse; being a good big data reviewer
