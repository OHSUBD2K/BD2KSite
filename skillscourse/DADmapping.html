Data After Dark (January 2016) Content Mapping

This short course was presented to an open audience of graduate students, research professionals, and administration staff interested in learning about Big Data. This mapping shows how the BD2K Open Educational Resources can align with the presented objectives for the Data After Dark course.

  • Learning Objective #1

    What is big data and how does it relate to your research?

    Learning Content Recommendations:

    • BDK01 - Biomedical Big Data Science
    • BDK02 - Introduction To Big Data In Biology And Medicine
    • BDK04 - Clinical Data Standards Related To Big Data

  • Learning Objective #2

    Learn about emerging NIH data science activities and requirements

    Learning Content Recommendations:

    • BDK05 - Basic Research Data Standards
    • BDK09 - Publication And Peer Review
    • BDK12 - Data Annotation And Curation
    • BDK19 - Replication, Validation And The Spectrum Of Reproducibility

  • Learning Objective #3

    Be more impactful with your science and your data

    Learning Content Recommendations:

    • BDK03 - Ethical Issues In Use Of Big Data
    • BDK28 - Translating the Question
    • BDK18 - Visualization And Interpretation

  • Learning Objective #4

    Do better research by leveraging big data concepts

    Learning Content Recommendations:

    • BDK24 - Computing Concepts for Big Data
    • BDK27 - Context-based selection of data
